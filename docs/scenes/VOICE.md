# Video-script voice guide (Zaid)

Source of truth for **how the spoken scripts should sound**. Derived from EA
`references/zaid-voice.md` (which covers *written* deliverables and has no spoken/video
mode yet). Adapted here for spoken video. Do NOT edit EA's ref from this project; add a
video mode there in an EA session later.

## What carries to audio (the rules that actually matter on tape)
Spoken delivery hides punctuation but exposes word choice.

- **No "X, not Y" cleverness punchlines.** Banned in his voice, and audible.
  - Bad: "Groundedness is enforced, not assumed." / "measured quality, not vibes."
  - Good: "The reviewer checks every claim against the evidence before the answer goes out."
- **No mechanical symmetric tricolons as closers** ("measured, traced, and live"). Say it plainly.
- **First person, declarative.** "I built," "I wrote," "I evaluate," "I can trace." He owns the work.
- **Short declaratives mixed with some longer flowing sentences.** Not every line is a dramatic fragment.
- **Concrete over abstract.** Name the tool, the number, the file.

## Script-file mechanics
- **No em-dashes in the script text.** Use separate sentences, periods, colons, or parentheses.
  (On tape it's just a pause, but on the page em-dashes pull toward AI-drama cadence.)
- **Keep:** "really," "actually," "I realized," "I believe that," "I came to understand" — signature Zaid.
- **Cut:** "leverage"/"utilize" (use "use"), "deep dive," "reach out," "seamless," "robust," and hedges ("kind of," "sort of").
- **Banned (from zaid-voice.md universal rules):** em-dash separators, "X, not Y" parallels, "Together, these..." / "In a nutshell" summary bridges, corporate filler.

## How each scene gets its voice
- **Framing scenes (1, 2, 11):** verbatim, voice-matched. **1 + 11 are already recorded** — their files match the footage and are not re-edited.
- **Technical scenes (3-10):** talking points only. Zaid reworks them into his own words at drill time (predict-first). That rework IS the voice match; lock his wording then.
