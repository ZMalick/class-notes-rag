In this video I'll walk through two major problems with large language models — and how my project solves both.

[the two problems]

LLMs have two well-known issues. First, they hallucinate — they'll confidently state things that aren't true. Second, their knowledge is frozen at their training date — anything that happened after it, the model simply doesn't know. For anyone doing real research, that combination is disqualifying — you need answers that are grounded in real sources and that stay current.

[the build + the how]

So I built a multi-agent Research Assistant. It's three agents running on a single Gemini model, working together to give a user reliable, grounded answers. For any question, it decides whether to pull from a library of AI/ML research papers, search the live web, or both — and it fact-checks its own answer against the evidence before you ever see it, with a citation on every claim.

Let me walk you through how it works, show it running live, and show it deployed."
